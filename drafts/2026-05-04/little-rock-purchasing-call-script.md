# Call Script — Little Rock Purchasing Department

**For:** Anthony Sons
**Prepared by:** Cassie (with Milli sales playbook backing)
**Segment:** Government / Municipal
**Goal of call:** Get on the City of Little Rock's approved vendor list and identify upcoming RFPs for exterior cleaning, fleet washing, parking lot maintenance, and construction final cleaning.
**Reference:** memory/sales-playbook.md → Government/Municipal angle + NEPQ qualifying questions

---

## Before You Dial — 60-Second Prep

- **Who you're calling:** City of Little Rock Purchasing Department (general purchasing — not a specific contact unless one is named)
- **City contact info to verify:** purchasing.littlerock.gov / 501-371-4570 (confirm before dial)
- **What we want:**
  1. Get on the City's approved/registered vendor list
  2. Find out which department issues RFPs for: exterior cleaning, fleet washing, parking lot maintenance, gutter work, construction final cleanup
  3. Get a contact name + email for each relevant department
  4. Confirm where bids are posted (AR Bid Online, city site, both)
- **What to have ready:** EIN, AR contractor's license #, COI on standby, W-9, list of services we provide

---

## Opening (Friendly, professional, no pitch yet)

> "Hi, this is Anthony Sons with American Services AR — we're a commercial cleaning and exterior maintenance company based in Conway. I'm trying to figure out how to get registered as a vendor with the City of Little Rock. Am I in the right place, or is there someone specific I should be talking to?"

**If transferred:** Restart with the same line at the new contact.

**If they say "yes, this is the right place":** Move to qualifying.

---

## Qualifying Questions (NEPQ — let them tell you the process)

Ask these in order. Pause and let them answer fully.

1. "What does your vendor registration process look like — is it a portal, a packet, or do I just need to email some documents over?"
2. "Once we're registered, how do you typically notify vendors when an RFP comes out — email list, posted on the site, both?"
3. "Are bids for City of Little Rock work posted on AR Bid Online, on the city's procurement page, or somewhere else I should be watching?"
4. "Which departments typically buy services like exterior building cleaning, fleet washing, or parking lot maintenance? Public Works? Fleet Services? Parks?"
5. "Is there a primary buyer or contract specialist I should connect with directly for those service categories?"
6. "Do you keep a list of pre-qualified small or local vendors that get notified first when smaller jobs come up — under the formal bid threshold?"
7. "What's the typical response time you need on quotes once an RFP goes out?"
8. "Is there anything about your process that vendors usually get wrong on their first submission so I can avoid that?"

---

## What to Listen For

| If they say... | That tells you... | Do this |
|----------------|-------------------|---------|
| "We have an online portal at [URL]" | Self-service registration | Get URL, register today |
| "You need to talk to [Name] in [Dept]" | Buyer-specific routing | Get name, email, phone, ask for warm transfer if available |
| "We post everything on AR Bid Online" | Buddy already monitors that | Confirm Buddy is on it |
| "We have a small/local vendor preference" | Edge for ASAR | Ask how to qualify |
| "Bids come out [seasonal pattern]" | Pipeline timing | Note in Buddy's bid calendar |
| "We require [X cert / X insurance / X bonding]" | Compliance hurdle | Capture exact requirements |

## If They Push Back

### "We already have a vendor for that."
> "Got it — completely understand. Just so I know how the city handles this: is that vendor on a long-term contract, or is it re-bid each year? And is there value in us being on the list as a backup if that vendor can't perform on a job?"

### "We don't really need anyone right now."
> "That's fair. Is there a time of year when the city typically goes to bid for exterior maintenance or fleet services so I can plan around that? And is there any harm in me getting registered now so we're ready when the time comes?"

### "Just go to the website."
> "Will do — I'll do that today. Quick question while I have you: is there one specific person who handles vendor questions if I run into something on the form, or should I just call back to this number?"

---

## Closing

Once you have what you need:

> "Really appreciate the time. Last thing — is there anything else you'd recommend a new vendor do to make sure we're top of mind when the right opportunity comes up? And if I get registered today, when should I expect to start seeing notifications?"

End with:

> "Thanks again, [Name]. I'll get this submitted today. If anything specific to exterior cleaning, fleet washing, or parking lot work crosses your desk in the meantime, my direct email is asons@americanservicesar.com — happy to hear from you anytime."

## Notes

- **Tone:** Curious, helpful, low-pressure. You're not selling — you're learning their process. Per NEPQ: "Tonality: curious, slightly confused, like a doctor asking about symptoms."
- **Don't pitch services.** This call is about getting on the list, not closing a job. Pitching too early kills government deals.
- **If they offer a tip, take it.** Government buyers know exactly how to win their bids — listen.
- **Once registered, Buddy owns the relationship.** All RFP responses route through him + Milli closes.
