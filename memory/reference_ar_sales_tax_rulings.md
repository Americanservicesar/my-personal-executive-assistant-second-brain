## Taxable Services (AR GR-9.4 / GR-9.7)
Cleaning services on real property are taxable in Arkansas:
- Pressure washing
- Roof washing
- Gutter cleaning
- Driveway cleaning
- Parking lot washing/cleaning
- Fleet washing (mobile)
- Any cleaning service on a structure

## Non-Taxable — Repair Labor (AR GR-18)
Labor to repair real property fixtures is NOT taxable. Only materials are taxable.
- Gutter repair (labor portion) = non-taxable
- Installation of gutters attached to building = debatable (fixture vs. tangible personal property)
- Key rule: if it's permanently attached to the real property structure = real property improvement = labor non-taxable

## Non-Taxable — Contractor Materials (AR Use Tax Exception)
If Anthony buys materials at retail (Home Depot, Lowe's, etc.) and:
1. Pays sales tax at point of purchase
2. Uses/consumes the materials in the job
3. Does NOT resell them separately to the customer

→ **No use tax owed. No customer tax owed. Completely clean.**

This is the legitimate defense for many "Non-Taxable" entries in QB. Jobs invoiced as repair/labor where he ate the materials and paid retail sales tax = defensible non-taxable.

## Services to Discuss with CPA
- Christmas light installation: likely taxable (tangible personal property + installation)
- Commercial parking lot services (line striping, sealcoating): mixed — labor may be non-taxable on real property, materials taxable
