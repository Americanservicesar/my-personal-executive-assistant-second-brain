## 1. SILO STRUCTURE THEORY

### What Is a Silo?

A silo is a method of organizing website content into clearly defined, thematically related groups. Each group (silo) covers one core topic exhaustively, with a parent page at the top and supporting child pages beneath it. The concept comes from information architecture and was popularized for SEO by Bruce Clay in the early 2000s, but remains the dominant best practice through 2026.

The principle: Google determines what a page is about not just from the page itself, but from the pages surrounding it, linking to it, and grouped with it. A page about "pressure washing in Conway" gains more authority when it lives inside a cluster of related pressure washing content than when it sits randomly alongside unrelated pages.

### Why Silos Work for SEO

**Topical Authority Signal.** Google's ranking systems (including the Helpful Content system and topic authority classifiers) evaluate whether a site demonstrates depth on a subject. A site with 15 pages all interlinked about pressure washing signals far more authority on "pressure washing" than a site with 1 page on 15 different topics.

**Crawl Efficiency.** Googlebot follows internal links. When related pages link to each other in a tight cluster, the crawler understands the semantic relationship. When unrelated pages link randomly, the topical signal gets diluted.

**Link Equity Concentration.** PageRank (internal link equity) flows through internal links. A silo concentrates that equity within a topic cluster, making every page in the silo stronger for its target keywords.

**User Experience.** Visitors who land on a pressure washing page want to see related pressure washing content, not gutter installation. Silos create logical navigation paths that reduce bounce rate and increase pages per session — both indirect ranking factors.

**Entity Recognition.** Google's Knowledge Graph and entity understanding systems identify topics more clearly when content is grouped. A silo helps Google build a stronger entity association between your brand and a service category.

### Physical Silos vs Virtual Silos

**Physical Silo (Directory-Based)**
Content is grouped by URL directory structure. The URL itself reflects the hierarchy.

```
/pressure-washing/                          (pillar page)
/pressure-washing/conway/                   (city page)
/pressure-washing/little-rock/              (city page)
/pressure-washing/commercial/               (sub-service)
```

Pros:
- Google parses URL structure as a relevance signal
- Clear hierarchy visible in URLs
- Easy to manage in WordPress with parent/child pages
- Breadcrumbs automatically reflect the silo

Cons:
- Deeper URLs (3+ levels) can slightly dilute crawl priority
- Restructuring requires 301 redirects

**Virtual Silo (Link-Based)**
Pages can live at any URL depth, but internal linking creates the silo relationship. The silo exists through link patterns, not URL structure.

```
/pressure-washing/                          (pillar)
/pressure-washing-conway/                   (flat URL, linked to pillar)
/commercial-pressure-washing/               (flat URL, linked to pillar)
```

Pros:
- More flexible — easier to reorganize without URL changes
- Works when CMS limitations prevent nested URLs

Cons:
- Requires disciplined internal linking (easy to break the silo accidentally)
- URL structure does not reinforce topical grouping

**RECOMMENDATION FOR ASAR: Hybrid approach.** Use physical silos (directory-based URLs) for the primary service > city structure, reinforced by strict virtual silo linking rules. This gives you both the URL signal and the link equity signal.

### Flat vs Deep Architecture

**Flat Architecture:** Every page is 1-2 clicks from the homepage. Good for small sites (<30 pages).

**Deep Architecture:** Pages are nested 3-4+ levels deep. Can bury important pages too far from the homepage.

**RECOMMENDATION: Moderately flat.** No page should be more than 3 clicks from the homepage. For ASAR:
- Click 1: Homepage > Service Pillar (e.g., /pressure-washing/)
- Click 2: Service Pillar > City Page (e.g., /pressure-washing/conway/)
- Click 3: City Page > Blog Post (e.g., /blog/pressure-washing-driveway-conway/)

This is the ideal depth for a local service business with 100-200 pages.

### How Internal Linking Creates Topical Authority

Internal links do three things:
1. **Pass PageRank** — every link passes a fraction of the linking page's authority
2. **Define relationships** — Google uses anchor text and link context to understand what the target page is about
3. **Establish hierarchy** — pages with more internal links pointing to them are treated as more important

When your homepage links to /pressure-washing/, and /pressure-washing/ links to 11 city pages, and each city page links back to /pressure-washing/, you create a closed topical loop. Google sees:
- The pillar page is the most important page for "pressure washing" (most inbound internal links)
- Each city page is a localized variant (contextual child links)
- The topic is deeply covered (11 supporting pages)

This is how a 50-page site can outrank a 500-page site that has no silo structure.

## 3. URL STRUCTURE

### The /service/city/ Format (RECOMMENDED)

**Use: /service-name/city-name/**
**Do NOT use: /city-name/service-name/**

#### Why /service/city/ Wins

1. **Topical clustering in URL directories.** When Google crawls /pressure-washing/conway/ and /pressure-washing/little-rock/, it sees them as children of /pressure-washing/. This reinforces that the parent directory "owns" the pressure washing topic. With /conway/pressure-washing/, the parent is "conway" — which dilutes the service topic signal.

2. **Keyword prominence.** The service keyword appears earlier in the URL, which carries slightly more weight in URL-based ranking signals.

3. **Scalability.** If you add 5 more cities later, you add 5 child pages under each service — clean and logical. With /city/service/, you'd need to create new city parent directories.

4. **Breadcrumb logic.** Home > Pressure Washing > Conway makes more sense to users (and Google) than Home > Conway > Pressure Washing. Users search by service first, location second.

5. **Industry standard.** The dominant pattern among top-ranking local service businesses in 2025-2026 is /service/city/.

### URL Rules for Every Page Type

| Page Type | URL Format | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Homepage | / | americanservicesar.com/ |
| Service Pillar | /service-name/ | /pressure-washing/ |
| Sub-Service | /service-name/sub-service/ | /pressure-washing/commercial/ |
| Service + City | /service-name/city-name/ | /pressure-washing/conway/ |
| Sub-Service + City | /service-name/sub-service/city-name/ | /gutter-services/gutter-cleaning/conway/ |
| Commercial Hub | /commercial/ | /commercial/ |
| Residential Hub | /residential/ | /residential/ |
| Service Area Hub | /service-areas/ | /service-areas/ |
| City Hub | /service-areas/city-name/ | /service-areas/conway/ |
| Blog Index | /blog/ | /blog/ |
| Blog Category | /blog/category-name/ | /blog/pressure-washing/ |
| Blog Post | /blog/post-slug/ | /blog/how-often-pressure-wash-driveway-arkansas/ |
| About | /about/ | /about/ |
| Contact | /contact/ | /contact/ |
| Reviews | /reviews/ | /reviews/ |
| Gallery | /gallery/ | /gallery/ |
| Financing | /financing/ | /financing/ |

### URL Formatting Rules

1. **Always lowercase** — /Pressure-Washing/ is wrong, /pressure-washing/ is correct
2. **Hyphens, never underscores** — /pressure_washing/ is wrong
3. **Trailing slash on all pages** — /pressure-washing/ not /pressure-washing
4. **No stop words unless natural** — /deck-fence-cleaning/ not /deck-and-fence-cleaning/ (shorter is better, but keep readability)
5. **No keyword stuffing** — /pressure-washing/ not /pressure-washing-services-company/
6. **State/region not needed in URL** — Google knows Conway is in Arkansas from your GBP, schema, and content. /pressure-washing/conway-ar/ adds nothing.
7. **Max 3 directory levels** — /gutter-services/gutter-cleaning/conway/ is the deepest. Never go 4+ levels.
8. **No dates in service page URLs** — dates belong only in blog post URLs if at all
9. **No page numbers or IDs** — /pressure-washing-2/ or /?p=123 are WordPress defaults that must be fixed

### Special Cases

**"North Little Rock"** — Use /north-little-rock/ (hyphens between all words)

**"Deck & Fence Cleaning"** — Use /deck-fence-cleaning/ (drop the ampersand, no special characters in URLs ever)

**Blog Posts** — Use descriptive slugs: /blog/5-signs-your-gutters-need-cleaning/ not /blog/gutter-cleaning-post-1/

## 4. INTERNAL LINKING RULES

### The Cardinal Rule

**Pages within a silo link to each other. Pages do NOT link to pages in other silos, except through hub pages or the homepage.**

This is the single most important silo discipline. Breaking this rule is the #1 way silos get destroyed.

### Link Flow Within a Silo

```
                    HOMEPAGE
                       |
                       v
              SERVICE PILLAR PAGE
              /pressure-washing/
               /    |    |    \
              v     v    v     v
          CITY   CITY  CITY  CITY
          pages  pages pages pages
          (each links back to pillar)
          (each links to 2-3 sibling city pages)
```

#### Rule 1: Pillar Page Links DOWN to All Its Children
The /pressure-washing/ pillar page must contain links to every city page:
- "Pressure Washing in Conway"
- "Pressure Washing in Little Rock"
- etc.

These links should appear naturally in a "Service Areas" section on the pillar page. Each link uses descriptive anchor text (not "click here").

#### Rule 2: Every City Page Links UP to Its Pillar
Every /pressure-washing/conway/ page must link back to /pressure-washing/ at least once, ideally 2-3 times:
- Once in the intro ("Learn more about our [pressure washing services](/pressure-washing/)")
- Once in breadcrumbs (automatic)
- Once in a CTA or related services section

#### Rule 3: City Pages Link SIDEWAYS to 2-4 Sibling City Pages
The /pressure-washing/conway/ page should link to 2-4 nearby city pages within the same silo:
- "We also serve [Little Rock](/pressure-washing/little-rock/), [Vilonia](/pressure-washing/vilonia/), and [Greenbrier](/pressure-washing/greenbrier/)"

Choose geographically adjacent cities. Do NOT link to all 10 other cities — that dilutes link equity. Rotate which cities link to which based on geographic proximity:

**Conway links to:** Vilonia, Greenbrier, Maumelle, Cabot
**Little Rock links to:** North Little Rock, Sherwood, Maumelle, Benton
**North Little Rock links to:** Little Rock, Sherwood, Jacksonville, Maumelle
**Sherwood links to:** North Little Rock, Jacksonville, Cabot, Little Rock
**Maumelle links to:** Little Rock, North Little Rock, Conway, Sherwood
**Benton links to:** Bryant, Little Rock, Maumelle
**Bryant links to:** Benton, Little Rock, Maumelle
**Cabot links to:** Jacksonville, Sherwood, Conway, Vilonia
**Jacksonville links to:** Sherwood, North Little Rock, Cabot
**Vilonia links to:** Conway, Greenbrier, Cabot
**Greenbrier links to:** Conway, Vilonia

#### Rule 4: Cross-Silo Links Go Through Hub Pages ONLY
The /pressure-washing/conway/ page should NOT directly link to /roof-cleaning/conway/. Instead:
- /pressure-washing/conway/ links to the /service-areas/conway/ hub page
- /service-areas/conway/ links to /roof-cleaning/conway/

This preserves silo integrity. The hub pages are the authorized "bridges" between silos.

**Exception:** A brief "Other Services in [City]" section at the bottom of a city page with 2-3 cross-silo links is acceptable if done with nofollow or sparingly. The key is that the MAJORITY of links on a page stay within its silo.

#### Rule 5: The Homepage Links to All Pillar Pages
The homepage is the root of all silos. It must link to every service pillar page:
- /pressure-washing/
- /soft-washing/
- /roof-cleaning/
- /concrete-cleaning/
- /deck-fence-cleaning/
- /window-cleaning/
- /gutter-services/
- /fleet-washing/
- /parking-lot-maintenance/
- /construction-cleanup/
- /holiday-lighting/

Plus hub pages:
- /commercial/
- /residential/
- /service-areas/

### Anchor Text Strategy

| Link Type | Anchor Text Style | Example |
|-----------|------------------|---------|
| Pillar > City | "pressure washing in Conway" | Exact match city variant |
| City > Pillar | "pressure washing services" or "our pressure washing team" | Partial match, natural |
| City > Sibling City | "serving Little Rock" or "pressure washing Little Rock" | Geo-modified |
| Homepage > Pillar | "Pressure Washing" or service name | Exact or branded |
| Hub > Silo Page | Service + City or service name | Descriptive |
| Blog > Pillar | Long-tail or informational | "professional pressure washing" |
| Blog > City Page | Long-tail + city | "pressure washing services in Conway, AR" |

**Rules:**
- Vary anchor text — never use the same exact anchor text for every link to the same page
- Never use "click here" or "learn more" as anchor text
- Include the target keyword naturally — Google reads anchor text as a description of the target page
- Keep anchor text under 6-8 words

### Internal Links Per Page — Targets

| Page Type | Internal Links Target | Notes |
|-----------|----------------------|-------|
| Homepage | 15-25 | All pillars, hubs, key pages |
| Service Pillar | 15-20 | All city pages + 2-3 blog posts + commercial/residential if applicable |
| Service + City Page | 8-15 | Pillar (up), 2-4 siblings, 1-2 blog posts, 1 hub page |
| City Hub Page | 12-18 | All services available in that city |
| Commercial/Residential Hub | 10-15 | All relevant service pillars |
| Blog Post | 5-10 | Parent pillar, 1-2 city pages, 1-2 related blog posts |

### Link Equity Flow Summary

```
HOMEPAGE (highest authority)
  |
  |--- distributes equity to 11 SERVICE PILLARS
         |
         |--- each pillar distributes to 11 CITY PAGES
                |
                |--- city pages link back UP (returns equity to pillar)
                |--- city pages link SIDEWAYS (distributes among siblings)

BLOG POSTS inject fresh equity into pillars and city pages via contextual links

HUB PAGES (commercial, residential, city hubs) act as bridges
```

## 5. PILLAR PAGE vs CLUSTER CONTENT

### Pillar Page (Service Pillar)

**URL:** /pressure-washing/
**Purpose:** Be the definitive resource for "pressure washing" as a topic. Rank for the head term "pressure washing [brand area]" and establish topical authority.

**Content Requirements:**

| Element | Target |
|---------|--------|
| Word Count | 2,500 - 4,000 words |
| H2 Sections | 8-12 |
| Images | 6-10 (real job photos, process shots, equipment) |
| Videos | 1-2 (process video, before/after) |
| Internal Links | 15-20 (all city pages, related blog posts) |
| External Links | 1-2 (to authoritative sources like PWNA, EPA) |
| FAQ Section | 8-12 questions |
| CTA | 3-4 throughout page |

**What Goes on a Pillar Page:**

1. Hero section — service name, primary city/region, CTA, hero image
2. Overview — what is pressure washing, why it matters, brief intro (200-300 words)
3. Services included — residential, commercial, what surfaces/areas
4. Process — step-by-step how you perform the service (builds trust + EEAT)
5. Equipment/methods — what PSI, what detergents, hot water vs cold, etc.
6. Benefits — why hire a pro vs DIY, property value, curb appeal, health
7. Surfaces we clean — driveways, sidewalks, patios, buildings, etc.
8. Before & After gallery — 4-6 real projects with captions
9. Pricing factors — what affects cost (do NOT put exact prices, use ranges or "factors")
10. Service areas section — links to all 11 city pages with brief descriptions
11. FAQ section — 8-12 common questions with schema markup
12. Final CTA — phone number, form, quote button

**What Does NOT Go on a Pillar Page:**
- City-specific content (that goes on city pages)
- Pricing calculators (separate page or blog post)
- Detailed competitor comparisons (blog post territory)
- Seasonal tips (blog post territory)

### Cluster Content (City Pages)

**URL:** /pressure-washing/conway/
**Purpose:** Rank for "[service] in [city]" and "[service] [city]" keywords. Provide localized content that the pillar page cannot.

**Content Requirements:**

| Element | Target |
|---------|--------|
| Word Count | 1,500 - 2,500 words |
| H2 Sections | 6-10 |
| Images | 4-8 (REAL photos from that city or nearby) |
| Internal Links | 8-15 |
| FAQ Section | 5-8 questions (city-specific) |
| CTA | 2-3 |
| Map Embed | 1 (Google Map showing your service area in that city) |

**What Goes on a City Page (Must Be Unique, Not Just City Name Swapped):**

1. Hero — "Pressure Washing in Conway, AR" + CTA + local hero image
2. City-specific intro — mention neighborhoods, landmarks, local references (200-300 words)
   - "Conway homeowners near Cadron Creek know that humidity and tree cover mean more algae buildup on driveways and sidewalks."
3. Services available in this city — brief list of what you do here
4. Local challenges — weather patterns, soil type, common exterior issues in this specific city
5. Recent projects — 2-3 before/after examples FROM this city (or clearly nearby)
6. Neighborhoods/areas served — list neighborhoods, ZIP codes, nearby streets
7. Why choose us for [city] — response time, local knowledge, years serving this area
8. FAQ — city-specific questions
   - "How much does pressure washing cost in Conway?"
   - "Do I need a permit for pressure washing in Conway?"
9. Nearby cities served — links to 2-4 sibling city pages
10. Link back to pillar — "View all our [pressure washing services](/pressure-washing/)"

**CRITICAL DIFFERENTIATION:** Each city page MUST have at least 40% unique content. If city pages are just templates with the city name swapped, Google will treat them as doorway pages and penalize them. Unique elements:
- Specific neighborhoods mentioned
- Local landmarks and references
- Real job photos from that city
- City-specific stats (population, housing age, common home styles)
- Different FAQ questions per city
- Different featured projects per city

### Pillar vs City Page — Quick Comparison

| Attribute | Pillar Page | City Page |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Primary Keyword | "pressure washing services" | "pressure washing Conway AR" |
| Word Count | 2,500-4,000 | 1,500-2,500 |
| Scope | Entire service, all areas | One service, one city |
| Geo-specific content | No | Yes (required) |
| Links to | All city pages, blog posts | Pillar, 2-4 sibling cities, 1-2 blogs |
| Schema | Service, LocalBusiness | Service, LocalBusiness, City-specific |
| Map embed | Optional (regional) | Required (city-level) |
| Gallery | Best-of from all areas | From this city only |

## 6. HUB PAGES

Hub pages serve as navigation connectors between silos. They do NOT replace pillar pages — they complement them.

### Service Area Hub Page

**URL:** /service-areas/
**Purpose:** Central page that links to all city hub pages. Ranks for "exterior cleaning near me" and "[brand] service areas."

**Content:**
- 800-1,200 words
- Interactive map showing all 11 cities served
- Brief description of each city with link to city hub page
- List of all services available across the region
- Driving radius / coverage area details
- Links to each individual city hub page

### City Hub Pages

**URL:** /service-areas/conway/, /service-areas/little-rock/, etc.
**Purpose:** Central page for EACH CITY that links to every service available in that city. Ranks for "exterior cleaning Conway AR" and "[brand] Conway."

**Content (per city hub):**
- 1,000-1,500 words
- All services offered in this city with links to each service+city page
- Brief city overview (population, housing stock, common exterior cleaning needs)
- Local map embed
- Testimonials from customers in this city
- Link to main /service-areas/ page

- /pressure-washing/conway/
- /soft-washing/conway/
- /roof-cleaning/conway/

City hub pages are critical because they are the AUTHORIZED cross-silo linkers. They connect different service silos through a geographic lens without breaking silo integrity.

### Commercial Hub Page

**URL:** /commercial/
**Purpose:** Aggregates all commercial-relevant services. Ranks for "commercial exterior cleaning Arkansas" and "commercial pressure washing central Arkansas."

**Content:**

- Overview of commercial capabilities

- Links to commercial-relevant service pillars:

## 10. COMMON SILO MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: Cross-Silo Linking Without Control
**What happens:** A page about pressure washing links directly to a page about gutter installation. Then gutter installation links to window cleaning. Then window cleaning links to holiday lighting. The silo walls are destroyed — Google sees a flat mess of pages with no topical grouping.

**Fix:** Cross-silo links only go through hub pages (city hubs, commercial hub, residential hub). Or if a direct cross-silo link is truly necessary (like a comparison blog post), limit it to 1-2 per page and never from a pillar or city page.

### Mistake 2: Doorway Pages (Duplicate City Pages)
**What happens:** You create 11 identical pages for pressure washing, changing only "Conway" to "Little Rock." Google recognizes these as doorway pages (thin pages designed solely to rank for location terms) and either ignores them or penalizes the entire domain.

**Fix:** Every city page needs 40%+ unique content. Real local photos, neighborhood names, city-specific references, different FAQ questions, different featured projects. If you cannot write unique content for a city, do NOT create the page.

### Mistake 3: Orphan Pages
**What happens:** A page exists but no other page links to it. Google may never crawl it, and even if it does, the page has zero internal link equity.

**Fix:** Every page must have at least 3 internal links pointing to it. Run a crawl audit monthly (Screaming Frog or RankMath's SEO Analysis) to find orphans.

### Mistake 4: Too-Deep Architecture
**What happens:** Pages are nested 4-5 levels deep (/services/exterior/pressure-washing/residential/conway/). Google's crawler gives diminishing importance to deeply nested pages. Users also rarely navigate that deep.

**Fix:** Max 3 directory levels. /gutter-services/gutter-cleaning/conway/ is the absolute limit.

### Mistake 5: Inconsistent URL Patterns
**What happens:** Some pages use /pressure-washing-conway/ (flat) while others use /soft-washing/little-rock/ (nested). Some use hyphens, others underscores. Some have trailing slashes, others don't.

**Fix:** Choose one pattern and enforce it across every single page. The standard: /service-name/city-name/ with hyphens and trailing slashes on all URLs.

### Mistake 6: Blog Posts Not Linked to Silos
**What happens:** Blog posts are written and published but never linked from service pillar or city pages. They exist in a disconnected blog section with no equity flowing to or from the silos.

**Fix:** Every blog post links to its parent pillar. Every pillar page has a "Related Articles" or "Learn More" section linking to 3-5 supporting blog posts.

### Mistake 7: Over-Optimization of Anchor Text
**What happens:** Every internal link to /pressure-washing/ uses the exact anchor text "pressure washing." This looks manipulative to Google.

**Fix:** Vary anchor text naturally. Use:
- "pressure washing services"
- "our pressure washing team"
- "professional pressure washing"
- "learn about pressure washing"
- "pressure washing" (occasionally, not every time)

### Mistake 8: Ignoring Cannibalization
**What happens:** The /pressure-washing/ pillar, /pressure-washing/conway/, and a blog post "Best Pressure Washing in Conway" all target the same keyword: "pressure washing Conway." Google doesn't know which to rank and may rank none.

**Fix:** Each page targets a DISTINCT primary keyword:
- Pillar: "pressure washing services" (no city)
- City page: "pressure washing Conway AR"
- Blog post: "how often pressure wash driveway Conway" (long-tail informational)

### Mistake 9: Footer Link Overload
**What happens:** The footer contains links to all 143 service+city combinations. This is a spam signal and dilutes link equity across too many URLs.

**Fix:** Footer links go to pillar pages (11) and city hub pages (11). That's 22 links, not 143.

### Mistake 10: No Internal Link Audit
**What happens:** Over time, new pages are added but never properly linked. Old pages have broken links. Some pages have 50 internal links, others have 1. The silo structure degrades silently.

**Fix:** Quarterly silo health audit:
- Run Screaming Frog crawl
- Check every page has 3+ inbound internal links
- Check no page has 50+ internal links
- Verify all cross-silo links go through hub pages
- Verify breadcrumbs match URL structure
- Verify no broken internal links (404s)
- Verify no redirect chains (301 > 301 > final page)

### Mistake 11: Creating Pages for Cities You Don't Actually Serve
**What happens:** You create city pages for 30 cities hoping to cast a wide net. But you've never done a job in half of them. No photos, no reviews, no real content. Google sees these as thin/fake pages.

**Fix:** Only create city pages for cities where you have (or will soon have) real work to showcase. Start with the 11 cities listed. Expand only when you have real photos and real customer reviews from a new city.

### Silo Health Audit Checklist

Run monthly or quarterly:

- [ ] Every service pillar links to all its city pages
- [ ] Every city page links back to its pillar
- [ ] Every city page links to 2-4 geographically adjacent sibling city pages
- [ ] No orphan pages (every page has 3+ inbound internal links)
- [ ] No broken internal links (0 404 errors)
- [ ] No redirect chains (max 1 redirect hop)
- [ ] Breadcrumbs on every page match URL hierarchy
- [ ] Blog posts link to relevant pillar and city pages
- [ ] Pillar pages link to 3-5 supporting blog posts
- [ ] Footer links go to pillars and city hubs only (not all 143 combos)
- [ ] No exact-match anchor text used more than 30% of the time for any single target
- [ ] Each city page has 40%+ unique content (not template-swapped)
- [ ] Schema markup present and error-free (test in Google Rich Results)
- [ ] XML sitemap up to date and includes all pages
- [ ] No pages deeper than 3 directory levels

| Old URL Pattern | New URL | Redirect Rule |
|----------------|---------|---------------|
| /pressure-washing-conway/ | /pressure-washing/conway/ | Single redirect |
| /services/pressure-washing/ | /pressure-washing/ | Single redirect |
| /conway-pressure-washing/ | /pressure-washing/conway/ | Single redirect |
| /gutter-cleaning/ | /gutter-services/gutter-cleaning/ | Single redirect |

**Redirect Implementation in WordPress:**

Option A: RankMath PRO Redirections module (built-in, easiest)
- RankMath > Redirections > Add New
- Enter old URL, new URL, select 301
- RankMath auto-detects 404s and prompts redirects

Option B: .htaccess (for bulk redirects)
```apache
# Pressure Washing city page redirects
Redirect 301 /pressure-washing-conway/ /pressure-washing/conway/
Redirect 301 /pressure-washing-little-rock/ /pressure-washing/little-rock/
Redirect 301 /conway-pressure-washing/ /pressure-washing/conway/
```

Option C: Redirection plugin (if not using RankMath PRO)

**CRITICAL REDIRECT RULES:**
1. Never chain redirects (A > B > C). Always redirect A directly to C.
2. Never redirect to a page that also redirects (verify target pages are live)
3. Keep redirects permanent (301, not 302)
4. Monitor 404 errors in Google Search Console for 90 days after migration
5. Keep the redirect rules in place for at least 12 months (preferably forever)
6. After redirecting, submit updated sitemap in Google Search Console
