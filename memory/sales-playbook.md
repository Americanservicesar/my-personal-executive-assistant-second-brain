## Core Sales Philosophy (NEPQ Framework)

Source: NEPQ Black Book of Questions -- Jeremy Miner, 7th Level Inc. (2024)
Full PDF: C:\Users\sales\Downloads\NEPQ+Black+Book+of+Questions+(PLEASE+DO+NOT+SHARE).pdf

- Never argue or defend. Arguing inflames resistance. Asking questions diffuses it.
- Internal tension beats external pressure. When YOU tell them why to buy, they resist. When THEY tell you, they believe it.
- 85% Engagement / 10% Presentation / 5% Commitment. Most of the call is questions, not pitching.
- Tonality: Curious, slightly confused -- like a doctor asking about symptoms. Not pushy.
- When they come to their own conclusions, they not only accept them -- they fight for them.

## Winning Pitch Angles (by Segment)

### Commercial Property Managers
Angle: "One call handles everything -- exterior maintenance, gutters, windows, parking lot, permanent lighting. One vendor, one invoice, one point of contact."
Why it works: PMs manage multiple vendors and hate coordination overhead. Consolidation is their win.
Open with: "What properties are you managing in Central AR right now?"

### Fleet / Logistics
Angle: "We come to your yard -- your drivers never leave, zero downtime, no detours to a fixed wash."
Why it works: Fleet managers are measured on uptime and compliance. Fixed truck washes kill uptime.
Open with: "How many units are you running, and what does your current wash process look like?"

### Apartments / HOA
Angle: "Clean property = fewer tenant complaints = better lease renewals. We handle the whole exterior."
Why it works: PMs are measured on occupancy and complaint volume. Curb appeal is their KPI.
Open with: "What does your turnover look like, and how much of that is driven by property condition?"

### Warehouses / Industrial
Angle: "OSHA compliance on dock pads + slip hazard prevention + quarterly maintenance window scheduling."
Why it works: Facility managers have safety targets and maintenance windows. We fit into their process.
Open with: "When is your next scheduled maintenance window?"

### General Contractors
Angle: "Final clean + COC done right, on schedule, every time. We show up."
Why it works: GCs need reliable subs. A no-show final cleaner kills punch-list and delays closing.
Open with: "What projects do you have in final clean phase right now?"

### Government / Municipal
Angle: "We are on AR bid portals and work within your procurement process. Certified, insured, compliant."
Why it works: Government buyers need vendor compliance above everything else.
Open with: "Are you currently sourcing through AR Bid Online or SAM.gov for this type of work?"

### Residential Upsell (In-Person at Time of Service)
Angle: "While we are here for the gutters, let us show you the driveway -- 15 minutes, included in today's visit."
Why it works: In-person upsell at time of service has the highest conversion rate.
Rule: Always quote Better or Best -- upsell is easier in person than by phone or email.

## Qualifying Questions (Use Before Proposing Anything)

NEPQ-based -- use in sequence to uncover the real situation before investing time.

1. "What is going on with [property/fleet/facility] right now that made you reach out?"
2. "How long has that been an issue for you?"
3. "What have you tried to solve it before? What happened?"
4. "How is that affecting [your tenants / your drivers / your operation] right now?"
5. "If you do not fix this, what happens in the next 6-12 months?"
6. "What would it mean for you personally if this got handled the right way?"
7. "Are you prepared to make a change so you can [repeat back what they said they wanted]?"
8. "Let us say we can solve [the specific problem] -- what do you see happening next?"

Qualifying red flags -- do not invest heavy time if:
- "Not sure, just getting quotes" (no pain identified)
- "I will have to run it by someone" without naming who or giving a timeline
- Cannot answer "what would you change if you could"
- No timeline for a decision

## Objections & Best Responses

### 1. "Too expensive / I can get it cheaper"
NEPQ: "I hear you. If the price was not a factor, is this something you would want to move forward with?"
- If yes: budget is the only issue -- solve it (phasing, timing, scope to hit $300 min)
- If no: deeper objection -- keep questioning
Follow-up: "What would it take for this to make sense for you?"
Last resort: "I can appreciate that money might be an issue. What other avenues do you have to find the funds so that you can [repeat back what they said they wanted]?"

### 2. "We already have someone who does that"
NEPQ: "Oh nice -- how long have you been with them?" [pause] "What do you like about working with them?" [pause] "What would you change if you could?"
The last question is the key. They will tell you their frustration. That is the opening.
Vendor disruption: "So it sounds like things are 100% perfect -- what would you change if you could?" (they almost always backpedal on "100% perfect")

### 3. "I need to think about it"
NEPQ: "Totally makes sense. What specifically is it you would want to think through?"
Follow-up: "Is it more about the timing, or is it something about what I shared that did not quite land right?"
Goal: Force them to name the real objection. Now you know what to solve.

### 4. "Send me a quote / Just give me a price"
NEPQ: "For sure -- just so I make sure the quote actually makes sense for your property, can I ask what you are looking to have done and roughly the size of the area?"
If they push: "What is prompting you to look at this right now?"
Rule: Never send a number cold -- it anchors them before they see value.

### 5. "Send info to my email, I will look at it"
NEPQ: "Yeah I can do that -- just so I send the right stuff, what specifically were you hoping to see? Ballpark pricing, before/afters, what we cover?"
After engagement: "Good -- so let me put together something specific to what you mentioned. Let us say you get it and it looks like it fits -- what would you want the next step to be?"
Goal: Get a commitment to a next step before sending anything.

### 6. "I will call you back / I am busy right now"
NEPQ: "Not a problem -- rather than playing phone tag, would it be easier if I sent you a link to grab a specific time? That way you do not have to chase me down either."
Follow-up: "With my schedule it is hard to randomly connect. If you have your calendar handy I can pull up mine -- would that work?"
Goal: Calendar commitment before ending the call.

### 7. "Not in the budget right now"
Response: Offer seasonal scheduling -- book now for next quarter at today's rate. Or phase the work.
NEPQ follow-up: "When does your budget cycle reset? What would need to happen for this to get allocated?"

### 8. "We handle it in-house"
Response: "What is your fully-loaded cost per hour for that crew including equipment, insurance, and management time? Most facilities save 30-40% outsourcing to us."
NEPQ add-on: "In what ways could your current process do better than it is doing now?"

### 9. "You are too small / We need a national vendor"
Response: "We are local -- which means when something comes up, we are 20 minutes away, not 3 days out on a work order. What does your current response time look like when something urgent comes up?"

### 10. "I need to get board / management approval"
Response: "Totally -- what information would they need to make a decision? I can put together a one-pager that makes it easy for you to get a yes."
NEPQ follow-up: "If they approve it, what is the timeline you are working toward?"
Goal: Stay in the deal and help them sell it internally.

### 11. "Your competitor charges less"
Response: "What did they include in that price? Our Better and Best packages include [specific extras] -- let me make sure we are comparing the same scope."
Rule: Never match a competitor price. Explain what we include that they do not.

## Proposal Rules

1. Never send a proposal without understanding their problem AND confirming they have budget.
   If they ask upfront: "I would be open to that -- but I am not sure we can even help you yet. It might make more sense if I asked a few quick questions first so the proposal fits your situation. Would that help?"

2. Proposals reference the 2-3 problems THEY stated. Not features. Not ASAR awards. Their pain.

3. Always offer 3 options (Good / Better / Best). Most land in the middle.

4. Always quote Better or Best first. Never quote below Good without Anthony approval.

5. $300 minimum on every job. Bundle services to hit minimum.

6. RoofSnap or GutterGlove measurements required before ANY gutter or roof quote.

7. Commercial = package pricing only. Never quote per-sqft to commercial clients.

8. Format: Brand header > Prepared for/by/date > Scope > Pricing Table > Included > Not Included > Timeline > Terms (50% deposit, Net 15 commercial, 30-day validity) > Why American Services AR.

9. Penn writes proposal copy. Milli sends via HCP. Penn does NOT send directly.
